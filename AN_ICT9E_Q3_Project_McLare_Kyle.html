5 ways to avoid fire accidents


Never leave your kitchen unattended whilst cooking.

Follow these guidelines when cooking and never leave food cooking unattended: Remain in the kitchen while food is cooking. Create a three-foot "kid-free zone" around your stove. Towels, paper, and other items that readily burn or catch fire should not be placed near your stove.

Maintain your wires

To prevent an electrical fire, learn to recognize the warning signs that indicate a possible wiring or electrical problem. Contact a licensed electrician immediately to examine and repair electrical problems if you experience any of the issues in your home.

Store flammable products correctly.

Ensure that all stored containers are in good condition, closed and properly labeled. Use flammable liquids and combustible liquids in a fume hood to prevent buildup of ignitable vapor/air mixtures or inhalation of toxic vapors or gases. Avoid placing ignition sources (hot materials, flames, or sparking equipment) in the general vicinity of these liquids. If possible, replace open flames by electrical heating.


Inspect your heating sources

Heating Devices. Most labs use at least one type of heating device, such as ovens, hot plates, heating mantles and tapes, oil baths, salt baths, sand baths, air baths, hot-tube furnaces, hot-air guns and microwave ovens.

Keep a fire extinguisher or blanket around at all times!

Put the fire blanket over the fire's origin as soon as possible. To make the fire source impermeable, the fire blanket should be briefly squeezed down all around. Due of the possibility of re-ignition, keep the extinguishing blanket covering the fire's source.
